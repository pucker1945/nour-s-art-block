This is a recreation of "Claude Monet" referenced from the original by John Singer Sargent

The story behind this is a rollecoaster from start to finish, Nour had gone on a school trip to a book fair where she met a girl called Malak. Malak was a very sweet girl who gave Nour and her friends free bookmarks and sketchbooks. After Nour showed Malak her art page, Malak insisted Nour signs up to an art course and in 1 month's time Nour was able to recreate this masterpiece in her own eyes.

this painting is called "Misery"

nour was very proud of herself for the way she captured the nose in this painting and how she magnificently captured the euphoria lighting. Despite her brother making fun of it she loved it even more

this painting is refrenced from the legendary samurai called "Miyamoto Musashi" as portrayed in the famous manga "Vagabond".

Despite her amazing talents she never once showed any signs of arrogance. This amazing portrait is in fact a birthday gift she worked so hard on just for a very dear and special friend. Nour has always been loved by everyone around and she never failed to show that love back

This painting is called "Suki"

The way nour was able to capture so much emotions in this one was just shocking, the way "Suki" has all the pretty looks and jewelry in the world even resembling the moon behind her yet her eyes show unimaginable emptiness behind them.

This painting is called "Jinx"

Nour on her many fun adventures she had joined a "Jinx" fan club online and was commissioned to make fan art of "Jinx", at that time Nour was on break and felt productive so she made lots of "silly" paintings, little did she know it turned into a fan favourite.

This painting is called "Smudged"

The name smudged comes from the literal tears of the artist, Nour had just come out of a bad physics exam and was only able to let out her frustration through her passion. As her tears fell on the portrait she decided to use that to project the bad mental health she had at the time and despite all that it turned out a masterpiece like the others

This painting is called "alternative"

Nour was trying to use a completely different colour palette than her usual and truly she never disappoints.

This portrait is called "Blossom"

It goes without saying everyone great still has their own failures otherwise we wouldn't learn, that's what Nour did with Blossom, she took the flower idea from an old painting she didnt like and tried going for an ethereal vibe and every painting just makes you fall in love all over again with her talent.

This painting is unnamed

It was a very impulsive painting, she wanted to combine elements that she loved which were fantasy themed girls and clouds and she has a very repetitive pattern with using clouds in her paintings, she thought the contrast of the white hair with the dark skin made a beautiful combination. and the transition from the hairline to the clouds, like colored people have such fluffy hair that reminded her of clouds. The diversity and beauty in her painting are simply jaw droping.
